Berlin: the city of contrasts

The average property prices vary significantly across different districts, influenced by factors such as location, amenities, and historical significance.

In the heart of Berlin, you can find stunning apartments in historic buildings, while the outskirts offer more affordable options. The city's diverse neighborhoods each have their own unique character, making it an exciting place to explore and invest in real estate.

Energy Source and Real Estate Value

Energy sources do more than power a home â€” they influence its value. Properties using solar, geothermal, or district heating typically command higher prices per square meter, reflecting a market preference for sustainable, energy-efficient infrastructure. These systems are common in newer or renovated buildings, appealing to both eco-conscious buyers and investors. In contrast, homes relying on LPG or electricity often show lower prices, likely due to higher operating costs and weaker energy performance. Energy choice is clearly a key factor in property value.
